# Diabetes Healthcare Expenditure Analysis (MEPS 2012–2022)

## Overview

This project analyzes healthcare expenditure trends among diabetes patients in the United States using the Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS) from 2012–2022.

The study focuses on identifying:
- Healthcare expenditure trends
- Insurance-related spending differences
- Key demographic and health-related expenditure drivers
- Statistical significance of healthcare cost factors

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyze long-term healthcare expenditure trends
- Identify major cost-driving variables
- Evaluate expenditure disparities across insurance groups
- Support evidence-based healthcare policy insights

---

## Dataset

- Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS)
- U.S. civilian noninstitutionalized population
- 22,431 individuals
- 1,420 variables

---

## Analytical Methods

- Survey-weighted regression analysis
- Expenditure trend analysis
- Stepwise variable selection
- Statistical significance testing
- Outlier and missing value handling

---

## Key Findings

- Healthcare expenditures increased significantly from 2012 to 2022
- Major expenditure drivers included:
  - Provider type
  - Cardiovascular disease
  - Health status
  - Race/Ethnicity
  - Insulin treatment

---

## Business & Healthcare Impact

This analysis provides insights into:
- Rising healthcare costs in diabetes management
- Insurance-related healthcare disparities
- High-cost medical risk factors
- Data-driven healthcare policy considerations
